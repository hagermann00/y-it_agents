## Income Distribution Statistics

Data from industry sample sets (n=500+) indicates a heavily skewed income distribution within the AAA model.

- 45% of participants generate $0 in revenue.
- 32% generate between $1 and $500 (pre-cost).
- 15% generate $501 to $2,000.
- Only 2% achieve revenues exceeding $10,000 per month.

## Barriers to Entry vs. Success Factors

The 2% of "Winners" share specific starting conditions:

- Average starting capital: $12,400.
- Average time commitment: 47 hours per week.
- Average months to reach first profit: 9 months.
These figures contrast with the marketed "zero down" and "passive" entry points.

## Market Resistance Factors

A significant constraint on the AAA model is institutional skepticism regarding AI reliability. Small business owners report high levels of "Hallucination Anxiety," where they are unwilling to allow automated systems to communicate with customers due to fear of reputational damage. This objection often requires a high-touch sales process that nullifies the efficiency of the "automation" model.

## Platform Risk

Agencies are dependent on third-party APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic). Changes in API pricing models or "model drift" (where a bot's performance degrades after a model update) create a continuous maintenance requirement that is often not accounted for in initial profit margin projections.
